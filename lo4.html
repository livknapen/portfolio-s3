Design Challenge /HMW-questions

To get a better overview of the awareness we want to make in our project, we prepared “How Might We” questions. Because of these questions, we have an overview of things we want to aware the users of. With all these questions we ultimately jointly developed two detailed questions that we will use for the project.


Summary process

For all my projects, I made a summary process to get a look back on how it went. I started with the telling about the concept, how it went, and what my conclusion is about the project.


Advisory report

There are a few tasks that we as a group could not complete in the time frame and which we advise our client to complete so that they can be completed. With this advice, the client could complete the project.


Ethical considerations

For the projects I made, I have some considerations that I was able to draw as a conclusion from my process. I could use this in the next project so that the process can run more smoothly until a final product.


Research questions and methods

I described all the research questions and methods I used in my projects, in my document. In my portfolio Abstract I have summarized these methods about why I used these methods and how I could add them to the projects.


Multiple DOT-framework areas

For all the methodes I used in my projects, I wrote a reflection on how I used this method and how I used this in the projects. Also, how this way is good for me.


Conclusions/Translations to project

For all my used methods of the DOT-framework, I wrote a conclusion and how I translated this into the projects. Ultimately, this gave me a good idea of ​​what I was able to get out of the methods and apply them in the projects.


Triangulation

For the AI project, I used a triangulation with 3 methods to do a research about the target audience. I made a Persona with an Empathy map, and we made a survey to get more information of what our audience knows about AI.


SCRUM

With the group we maintained a Trello board where someone was the SCRUM master and kept track of the sprints. Every morning, we have a standup where everyone indicates what they have done and will do. This allowed us to support each other when necessary. I like to work with Trello, it is very organized and in one blink you can see if something is done or still in progress.
